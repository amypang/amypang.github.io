I'm interested in algebraic structures on combinatorial objects, and their interplay with Markov chains. My current research themes are

Markov chains from linear operators on combinatorial Hopf algebras
I aim to keep here a comprehensive, reader-friendly reference on this topic. Please let me know of typos and confusing wording so I can fix them, and any new work in the area that I should add.

Papers in reverse chronological order (of authorship):

Markov Chains from Descent Operators on Combinatorial Hopf Algebras (2016) a preprint on arXiv. A reader-friendly overview, known as "Card-Shuffling via Convolutions of Projections on Combinatorial Hopf Algebras" was an extended abstract and talk for FPSAC 2015 (last update: July 28, 2015).

Lumpings of Algebraic Markov Chains arise from Subquotients (2017), a preprint on arXiv. Lumping theorems for linear-algebraic and Hopf-algebraic Markov chains. A first version, released 2015, was entitled "A Hopf-Algebraic Lift of the Down-Up Markov chain on Partitions to Permutations".

Hopf Algebras and Markov Chains (2014), revised version of my thesis. This (and everything below) is one special case of the theory, using the coproduct-then-product operator.

A Hopf-power Markov chain on compositions (2013), an extended abstract and poster for FPSAC 2013. Superceded by section 6.2 of my thesis.

Hopf Algebras and Markov Chains: two examples and a theory (2014), joint with Persi Diaconis and Arun Ram, in Journal of Algebraic Combinatorics, and latest update (30 December 2014). This is the original paper on this method of making Markov chains from Hopf algebras. Almost everything is superceded in my thesis.
