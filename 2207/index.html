  • This schedule is tentative and subject to change throughout the semester. (We will try to go a little faster than what's written.)
  • The stars indicate the rough difficulty of the topic. The numbers indicate the relevant sections of the textbook (5th edition).
  • The lecture slides are outlines only, they do not contain everything covered in lectures, and reading them is not a substitute for coming to class.
  • You are strongly recommended to quickly read the lecture slides and textbook sections before class, and to print and bring the lecture slides to class, so you can make notes on them, and easily refer to previous slides during class.

  • The dates in gray below are tentative and subject to change. It is the student's responsibility to check this page for new homework postings.
  • Homework is due in class, 15 minutes after the start of class, on the due date. Please place your work on the piles near the entrances of the classroom. No homework will be accepted after the due time, and no extensions will be granted under any circumstances.
  • You are encouraged to work with your classmates, but you must write up your solution by yourself - this is to ensure that you understand the solution, and can solve similar problems on the exams.
  • There are no marks for optional problems, and the material is not in the syllabus. They are for students who want to learn something extra.
  • Please staple all sheets of your homework together, and put your name and student ID clearly on the front page.
  • You are strongly encouraged to make a copy of your homework (e.g. take a picture with your phone) before handing it in, so you can compare your work to the official solutions as soon as possible after the deadline.
  • You can collect your graded homeworks and in-class exercises anytime from the box outside Dr. Pang's office.
