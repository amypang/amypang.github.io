You may only take the quizzes in the tutorial in which you are registered. The quizzes for each tutorial are entirely different. If you even attempt a quiz in another tutorial, you will automatically receive a score of zero. There will be no makeup quiz whatsoever. It is your responsibility to contact your section leader to retrieve your graded quizzes.

The quizzes are substantial: they last 30 - 40 minutes, and will be graded in the same way as on the final.

The quizzes and the final are closed book exams. No calculators will be allowed, but you don’t need to simplify any arithmetic unless explicitly told to do so in the question.

About 10 days before each quiz, some hints will be posted through MyCourses, and reposted here. You are encouraged to set up subscriptions to postings on MyCourses.
Q: Will there be lecture notes / lecture recordings?
A: No. I know some people can't get to class on time because of the location, but if I put out lecture notes / lecture recordings, it will be too tempting to procrastinate, and calculus is not something you can cram at the last minute. I want you to do well in the class, so I'm going to motivate you to keep up with the material.

Q: Is it ok to use the 6th or 7th edition of the textbook?
A: If it is the Early Transcendentals Edition, you should be fine. The advantage of a newer edition is that it has more problems for you to practise on. Make sure you check with someone with the 8th edition whenever we mention a page, question, or section, since the numbering will definitely be different in older editions.

Q: This class is required for my major, but I know integration already, do I really have to take it?
A: You can take a placement exam and be exempt from the class. It seems like the application deadline has long passed, but speak to an academic advisor and see if something can be worked out.

Q: Will calculators be allowed in quizzes or the final?
A: No. But you don’t need to simplify any arithmetic unless explicitly told to do so in the question.

Q: Will there be formula sheets in the quizzes or the final?
A: You will get a few trig identities and summation formulae, but no antiderivatives will be provided, nor formulae for the various volume and area calculations. If you don't yet remember your antiderivatives, you haven't done enough practice problems to prepare for the exam.
