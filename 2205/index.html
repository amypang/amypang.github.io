Handwritten parts from class

  • This schedule is tentative and subject to change throughout the semester. (We will try to go a little faster than what's written.)
  • The stars indicate the approximate difficulty of the topic. The numbers indicate the relevant sections of the textbook (8th edition).
  • The lecture slides are outlines only, they do not contain everything covered in class (remember that in-class exercises are equally important!), and reading them is not a substitute for coming to class.
  • You are strongly recommended to quickly read the lecture slides and textbook sections before class, and to print and bring the lecture slides to class, so you can make notes on them, and easily refer to previous slides during class.

  • The dates in gray below are tentative and subject to change.
  • Homework is due in class, in the first 15 minutes after the start of class on the due date. No extensions will be granted under any circumstances.
  • You are encourged to work with your classmates, but you must write up your solution by yourself - this is to ensure that you understand the solution, and can solve similar problems on the exams.
  • Please staple all sheets of your homework together, and put your name clearly on the front page.
  • You are strongly encouraged to make a copy of your homework (e.g. take a picture with your phone) before handing it in, so you can compare your work to the official solutions as soon as possible after the deadline.
  • You can collect your graded homeworks and in-class exercises anytime from the box outside Dr. Pang's office.
