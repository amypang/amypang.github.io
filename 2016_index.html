You may only take the quizzes in the tutorial in which you are registered. If you even attempt a quiz in another tutorial, you will automatically receive a score of zero. There will be no makeup quiz whatsoever.

If you have logistic issues for the quizzes or the final (e.g. you are writing at the OSD), please contact Dr. Pang directly, instead of through your TA.

The quizzes and the final are closed book exams. No calculators will be allowed, but you donâ€™t need to simplify any arithmetic unless explicitly told to do so in the question. One exception: you shouldn't express your final answer as a composition of a trigonometric function and an inverse-trigonometric function - e.g. please write x/sqrt(1+x^2) instead of sin(arctan x). If the question asks for simplification, then trignometric values at multiples of pi/6 and pi/4 need to be evaluated.

All WebWork questions are fair game for the quizzes and final. However, there will also be questions unlike those on WebWork, which will require you to think on your feet. All questions will be solvable using material from lectures, tutorials, and WebWork. You will never be asked to solve the questions using a particular method. You may use any mathematically-valid method of your choice, (including material from outside the class, as long as you explain it fully).

The final is 3 hours long, and has two parts. In Part A, only your final answer will be scored; there is no partial credit. In Part B, you are expected to show all of your work and justify your answers; there will be partial credit available. There are no multiple choice questions.

The quizzes last 30 - 40 minutes. Unless noted otherwise, the questions on the quizzes are of a similar standard to the final, and will be graded in the same way. They will require you to show all your work, and there will be partial credit available.

The questions on the quizzes for each tutorial are entirely different, but they share the same format. About 10 days before each quiz, the common format will be released through MyCourses, and reposted here. You are encouraged to set up subscriptions to postings on MyCourses.

Solutions to quiz questions will be posted here and on MyCourses shortly after all students have taken the quiz.

The quizzes and final are written by Dr. Pang. (Dr. Pang did not write any questions for the calculus classes she taught previously at Stanford University, which use a different textbook, and the questions for this course are not based on those.)

The quizzes are graded by the TAs, though not necessarily your own TA, and the final is graded by Dr. Pang and the TAs. It is your responsibility to contact your TA to retrieve your graded quizzes.
